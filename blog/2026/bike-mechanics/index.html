How Bikes Work

I recently came across Bartosz Ciechanowski's blog post about how bicycles work and was inspired to do my own companion series alongside his. While his article focuses more on the physics of how bikes work, I want to focus more on their mechanical aspects.

While bikes are generally "simple" machines, modern bicycles have a good bit of clever engineering that I think is worth exploring. In the first post, I'll explore the individual components of what powers a bike and how that creates a model of the simplest version of a bike: The "fixie". Later posts will expand on bicycle engineering by adding more features to our model of what a bike is and how the mechanics change as more real-world constraints and elements are introduced to it.
